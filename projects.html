Projects & Research

Canadian Income Tax Calculator

After recieving my first pay-stub, I was curious about how income tax was calculated. I decided to investigate the Canadian income tax system further and I thought it would be a good idea to create lightweight mobile application. This would serve as a tool to those who want to quickly estimate their post-tax salaries.

I became familiar with the BlackBerry 10 (C++ / Qt) development platform and launched the application on native BlackBerry. But after switching my personal phone to Android, I decided to develop native Android (Java) version as well. I eventually played around with the Ionic Framework, and decided to re-build the app using Ionic 2 (TypeScript & Angular). I launched the app on iOS recently, and ported the hybrid app onto Android. Since the app's inception, it has garnered over 50,000 cross-platform downloads.

Symmetric Predictive Estimators (Neural Networks)

I enrolled into a Master's of Computer Science program at the University of Waterloo to pursue my interests in Computational Neuroscience and machine learning. My thesis focused on Predictive Estimators, a theory of how the brain processes bottom-up and top-down information. My professor Jeff Orchard, and I proposed a Symmetric Predictive Estimator, a biologically plausible model of how the brain perceives information from sensory input (e.g. eyes), and top-down information (e.g. conciousness). We used the spiking neural framework written in Python, called Nengo, which powers Spaun, the world's largest functional brain model.

This research was published in the IEEE Transactions on Neural Networks and Learning Systems, and presented at COSYNE 2016 (Computational and Systems Neuroscience).

IEEE Paper COSYNE Poster
Simultaneous learning of forward and backward identity functions in a biologically plausible, spiking neural network.

Brain Decoding of Mice Hippocampus

The 2014 Nobel Prize Physiology or Medicine were awarded to a group of scientists who were able to decode brain signals in mice and relate them to a location inside a maze. This discovery led to the Hippocampus playing a role of an internal GPS. In general, based on neuron firing patterns, it is possible to determine (or read the mind) of where a mice is in a maze.

Using similar datasets, I decided to benchmark various machine learning techniques and evaluate the accuracy and speed each technique has when decoding neural signals in the hippocampus. I tested custom K-Nearest Neighbor algorithm, as well as a tuned SVM classifier.

Whitepaper

Using neural signals from mice's Hippocampus, you can decode the mice's interal map, and determine position.
